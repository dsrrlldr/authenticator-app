Before you go

Having trouble? Let's fix it together

Most people uninstall because of one of these common issues — chances are it's a quick fix. Take a look before you say goodbye.

For your security, Authenticator app locks itself after a period of inactivity (auto-lock), so your codes stay hidden if you step away from your computer. You can change how long it waits before locking in Settings → Auto-lock timeout (1, 5, 10, 30 or 60 minutes) — pick a longer timeout if you'd like to be asked less often. Either way your vault stays encrypted on your device.

We never store or have access to your password, so it can't be reset or recovered from our side. A few things to check:

1. Make sure Caps Lock is turned off.
2. If you copy the password from a file, an extra space or line break may be copied with it — paste it into a text editor first to confirm it's exactly correct, or try typing it manually instead of pasting.
3. Recall any recent password changes or variations you might have used.
4. Reinstalling your operating system, Google Chrome, or the extension can clear locally stored data — if that happened, the vault may have been reset.

If none of these help, it's worth trying the different variations you may have used.

TOTP (Time-based One-Time Password) generates a unique 6-digit code every 30 seconds using a shared secret key and the current time. When you enable 2FA on a service, it gives you a QR code containing that secret. Authenticator app reads the QR code and generates the matching codes — no internet connection required.

Your account secrets are encrypted with AES-GCM-256. The encryption key is derived from your master password using PBKDF2 with 500,000 iterations. Your data never leaves your device and is never sent to any server.

TOTP codes are time-based — if your device clock is even slightly off, the generated code won't match the server. Make sure your system time is set to automatic sync (Settings → Date & Time → Set automatically). This is the most common cause of rejected codes.

Authenticator app stores all data locally in your browser's storage — nothing is sent to the cloud. Uninstalling Chrome or using a different device will not carry your accounts over. Always export an encrypted backup before reinstalling or switching devices, and import it afterward.
